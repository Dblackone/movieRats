Series to watch this quarantine

10. Bulletproof

  • Two Seasons
  • Sky One

Review- the show is about two cops pike and bishop who are best friends and how they solve crime in the streets of London. The first season wasn’t that impressive but the second season is why it is receiving a 4.6 and a recommendation. The show is excellent in portraying communication between the two leads and that conversation is often heart-warming and very funny. Would recommend.

9. Sick of it

  • Two Seasons
  • Sky One

Review – it is not everyday that you see a drama like karl pilkington’s sick of it. The show is about a man trying to get over a breakup and simply just taking life not too seriously. His closest friend is his inner self who talks boldly about the things that karl himself might not be able to say on his own. It is funny, heart-warming and excellently crafted. Would highly recommend.

8. His Dark Materials

  • One Seasons
  • Sky One

Review- if you are a fan of the golden compass the movie, this is a must watch for you. The show is about a girl lyra who is touted as a child of destiny and her adventures on realising this destiny. It involves a talking bear, an alethiometer ( a compass-like thing that can predict the future and find an answer to any question), witches and daemons ( the soul of everyone in this world has an animal representation called daemons). It’s a good watch and it will only get better

7. All Americans

  • Two Seasons
  • CW

Review – out of all the jargons that cw produced recently. All American is a standout drama and we had to put it on our list. The show is about a teenager who plays American football, his life and the people in his life. the show has a lot of heart and can be really emotional with topics ranging from sexuality to community. It’s a good watch and we highly recommend. Fun fact about this show. The guy that played spencer james is Daniel Ezra who is british but his American accent was really good

6. The Mandalorian

  • One Seasons
  • Disney+

Review- Disney’s entrance into the streaming business began when they debuted with the Mandalorian. It’s a story from the star wars universe and it centers around a Mandalorian ( played by pedro pascal) and his journey as a bounty hunter throughout the galaxy. The plot is simple, action- packed and really deliberate in its execution. We have spoken

5. The Peaky Blinders

  • Five Seasons
  • BBC America and Netflix

Review- the show is about a family out of Birmingham known as the Shelbys and how they want to move up in the world. Coming back from world war 1, Tommy Shelby ( the genius known as cilian murphy) and his brothers plan to use all means necessary to make a name for themselves. The show is gritty, violent and the theme is really catchy. with permission from the order of the peaky blinders, I write this review telling you guys to stream peaky blinders, watch out for the blade in the cap and the red right hand

4. Kingdom

  • Two Seasons
  • Netflix

Review- Zombies Zombies and more Zombies, who doesn’t like a good zombie movie or series. If you are tired of following the pioneer zombie show (the walking dead) and you want a fresh spin on the zombie genre try kingdom. Set in korea, it is about a conspiracy by a clan who in there bid to protect their power, revived the king from death using a plant known as the resurrection plant, only for their plan to backfire because the plant turned the king into a flesh eating zombie. A full blown pandemic came about from this single decision. Get ready to meet intriguing characters whose goal is to stay alive and protect the entire kingdom from perishing.

3. The Watchmen

  • one Seasons
  • HBO

Review- this was a huge bone of contention between us because I feel it should be 4.5 because of how outstanding this show is. Forgive HBO (we know the season 8 was woeful). Watch this show with an open mind and allow yourself be engrossed in a plot un like no other. Faithful to the watchmen universe and comics, the show talks about the lives of sister knight, Dr manhattan and other members of the watchmen. The show portrays a world where chaos can be expected at any time with such deep and meaningful conversation plot and aesthetical cinematography.

2. The Handmaids Tale

  • Two Seasons
  • Netflix

Review- the handmaids tale to us is one of the most underrated series of all time. The story is about a world where women do not have or own rights at all. Set in a fictional country known as GILEAD, due to the fact that there is wide barrenness in the world, GILEAD decided to overthrow the U.S government and create their own government based on selected parts of the bible and force fertile women into slavery and servitude. The show is gut-wrenching, brutal and can be so difficult to watch sometimes but that is credit to the fantastic acting (from Elizabeth moss) and good character development. UNDER HIS EYE..

1. The Boys

  • One Season
  • Amazon

Review- the superhero genre particularly with television has become really boring as networks like the CW keep green-lighting awful superhero shows. Enters amazon prime, they took a gamble on a this show and it paid off big time. Due to no affiliation with either marvel or DC, there is free reign to use and develop each character to there absolute limit. The boys is a dark take on what it means to be a hero and it is filled with beautiful character development with hilarious scenes culminating in an interesting premise from the pilot to the last episode. Grab a popcorn for this one, you are in for a ride
